<p><p>The PTCA guide wire market comprises primarily two types: solid steel wires and nitinol core wires. Solid steel wires are known for their stiffness and strength, making them suitable for straightforward procedures, while their rigidity can limit flexibility. Nitinol core wires, made from a nickel-titanium alloy, provide superior flexibility and elasticity, adapting to complex vascular anatomies. These characteristics make nitinol wires more favorable for intricate interventions, enhancing maneuverability while maintaining structural integrity during use.</p></p>
