<p><p>The Cleanroom HVAC market comprises two primary types: decentralized and centralized systems. Decentralized systems are designed for smaller cleanroom environments, where each room has its own individual unit, allowing for precise control of air quality and temperature. In contrast, centralized systems manage the HVAC needs of multiple cleanrooms from a single location, promoting efficiency and easier maintenance. Each type offers distinct advantages in terms of scalability, control, and operational efficiency, tailored to the specific requirements of cleanroom applications.</p></p>
