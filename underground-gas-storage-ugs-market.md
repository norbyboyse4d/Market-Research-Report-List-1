<p><p>Underground Gas Storage (UGS) involves storing natural gas underground to manage supply and demand fluctuations. The primary market types include depleted fields, aquifers, and salt caverns. Depleted fields utilize exhausted oil or gas reservoirs, leveraging existing infrastructure. Aquifers involve porous rock formations filled with water, where gas is injected under pressure. Salt caverns are created by solution mining of salt deposits, offering high flexibility and quick response times for gas withdrawal. Each type offers distinct advantages for energy security and market stability.</p></p>
